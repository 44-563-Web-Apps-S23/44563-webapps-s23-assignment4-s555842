It is one act,but there is more
LUKA.

Oh, ma'am, some one is asking for you, insists on seeing you----

MRS. POPOV.

You told him that since my husband's death I receive no one?

LUKA.

I said so, but he won't listen; he says it is a pressing matter.

MRS. POPOV.

I receive no one!

LUKA.

I told him that, but he's a wild man; he swore and pushed himself into the room; he's in the dining-room now.

MRS. POPOV.

[_Excitedly._] Good. Show him in. The impudent----!

[LUKA _goes out, centre_.]
MRS. POPOV.

What a bore people are! What can they want with me? Why do they disturb my peace? [_She sighs._] Yes, it is clear I must enter a convent. [_Meditatively._] Yes, a convent.
